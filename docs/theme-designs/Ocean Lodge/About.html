Our story

It started with a summer that never quite ended

The Daniels family has been on Camps Bay beach for three generations — first with a towel and a cooler box, then with a tired little apartment block we couldn't bear to see knocked down.

In 2014 we reopened it as Oceans View: forty-eight rooms, three pools, and a single rule — that nothing should ever come between a guest and the sea. We did the building slowly, by hand, keeping the bones and opening up every wall we could to the light and the water.

Today it's run by the same family, with a team who mostly grew up within walking distance. We still think of it less as a hotel than as our house on the beach, with a few more bedrooms than most.

The long version

Twelve years, one shoreline

From a family cooler box on the sand to forty-eight rooms above it — the slow, hand-built story of how Oceans View came to be.

2011

A block we couldn't lose

The Daniels family buys a tired little apartment block on Camps Bay beach, one storm away from the wrecking ball.

2014

Oceans View opens

Rebuilt by hand over three summers, keeping the bones and opening every wall we could to the light and the water.

2019

Off the grid by day

Solar goes in across the roof, the single-use plastic goes out, and the monthly beach clean-up becomes a house tradition.

2026

Still the same family

Three pools, two restaurants and 1,300+ guests a year later — run by the same people who grew up on this sand.
