What we make downstairs

Kira Mendes · Co-founder & Maker2 June 20266 min read

The print above your bed, the cup by your sink, the little ceramic dish for your rings — we made those. Not as a concept. Actually made them, by hand, ten metres below where you're sleeping.

Atelier was a design studio before it was a hotel, and the studio never left. It's still there, off the lobby, with the kiln and the press and the perpetual mess. Most afternoons you can wander in, watch us get it wrong a few times, and walk out with something if you like it enough.

Made, not bought

Hotels usually furnish from a catalogue — the same lamps, the same anonymous art, the same everything, in a thousand identical rooms. We find that quietly depressing. So instead we make as much as we can ourselves, and buy the rest from makers we know by name, mostly within a few streets.

"A room full of made things feels different to sleep in. You can't quite say why. You just rest better."— Kira Mendes

Why no two rooms match

It drives our accountant mad, but no two of our rooms share a palette, a layout or a single piece of art. Each one gets designed as its own small thing. It's slower, it's more expensive, and it means we can't ever photograph "a typical room" — because there isn't one.

  • Prints, ceramics and textiles made in the ground-floor studio.
  • The rest sourced from Cape Town makers we know personally.
  • Every room designed individually — no two alike, on purpose.

If something in your room catches your eye, ask at the desk. There's a fair chance the person who made it is downstairs right now, covered in clay, happy to tell you far more about it than you wanted to know.
