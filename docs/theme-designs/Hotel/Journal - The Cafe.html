The café that started it all

Riaan de Wet · Café Manager22 May 20265 min read

People assume the café came with the hotel. It's the other way around. There was a café, it got a little too popular, and the only way to grow was up. So they built bedrooms on top of the coffee.

It started as eight seats and a secondhand machine. Kira and Sol ran it between design jobs, mostly as an excuse to drink good coffee all day. Then the queue started forming down Loop Street, and the spare floor above suddenly looked less like storage and more like opportunity.

Coffee first, always

We still treat the café as the heart of the place, not an amenity. The beans are roasted a few streets away and change with the season. The milk's from a single farm in the Overberg. We pull every shot like someone's watching, because on Loop Street, someone usually is.

"A hotel can fake a lot of things. It can't fake a good flat white. Get that right and guests forgive you almost anything."— Riaan de Wet

What to order

Guests get breakfast downstairs, and I'd gently steer you away from the obvious. Here's the short version of a long opinion:

  • A cortado, not a flat white — the ratio's just better.
  • The seasonal filter, black, if you actually want to taste the coffee.
  • Whatever the kitchen baked that morning. Ask. It won't last past ten.

Come down early, before the street wakes up, and take the window seat. That's where Kira and Sol sat with their secondhand machine and a half-formed idea, a few years and twenty-two rooms ago. It's still the best seat in the house — and you don't even need to be staying with us to have it.
