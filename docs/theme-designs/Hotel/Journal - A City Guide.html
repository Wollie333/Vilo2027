A city guide for people who hate city guides

Sol Mendes · Co-founder12 June 20267 min read

I've lived in this city my whole life and I still can't read a Cape Town guide without wincing. So here's the opposite: no lists, no queues, just the few places I'd actually send a friend.

The thing every guide gets wrong is the same thing: it sends you to where everyone else already is. The real city is quieter and one street over. You don't need to see all of it. You need to see a little of it properly, with time to sit down.

Start on your own street

Loop Street, our street, is the easiest first day in town. Walk south for the design shops and the coffee, north for the galleries and the quiet end. Have breakfast in our café — I would say that, but it's true — and then just wander. You'll learn more in an hour on foot than a day on a bus.

"See half of the city twice, not all of it once. The second look is where it gets good."— Sol Mendes

The few things worth the trip

When you do venture further, three things genuinely earn it. Go early, before the wind and the crowds, and you'll understand why people move here on a whim.

  • Table Mountain on the first cable car of the day, before the cloud.
  • Kirstenbosch on a clear morning, with coffee and no plan whatsoever.
  • A slow drive to the winelands at dawn — let the desk sort the car.

And the tables? The best ones book out weeks ahead, and the ones that don't usually aren't worth it. That's what the front desk is for. Tell us what you feel like, and we'll have something held before you've unpacked. Then spend the rest of your trip ignoring this list entirely. That's allowed too.
