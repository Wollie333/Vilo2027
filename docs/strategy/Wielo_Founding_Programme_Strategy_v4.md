## 0. THE ONE-PARAGRAPH VERSION

Wielo recruits a small, capped group of South African accommodation Facebook group owners as **Founding Partners**. Each gets a co-branded landing page, a done-for-you marketing pack, and a lifetime recurring commission on every host they ever refer. They invite their members into the **Wielo Beta** — full access to the booking management system, the curated directory, and the Looking For demand board, at no charge, for four months from each host's own signup date. Hosts give structured feedback and vote publicly on what gets built next. At the end of their four months, each host is offered a Founding price locked below list for as long as they stay. Founding pricing exists only while Wielo is in beta. An 8-month competition runs alongside it, scored on activated and converted hosts, with three prizes. Wielo gets market entry, a real feedback loop, public proof it listens, honest reviews, and a directory that fills itself.

## 1. WHAT'S IN THE BETA — AND WHAT ISN'T

This scope decision drives every message in the programme. State it plainly everywhere.

| **IN — available from day one** | |
|---|---|
| **Full booking management system** | Reservations, rates, availability, calendar, guest records, direct messaging. Zero commission on every booking. |
| **Curated Wielo directory listing** | Discovery, zero commission. Founding Hosts get a permanent Founding badge and priority ranking. |
| **Looking For** | Guests post what they need — dates, area, budget, sleeps. Hosts respond with a quote. Demand delivered to the host. |
| **Full guest data** | Name, email, phone, booking history, on every booking. The emotional core of the product. |
| **The Build Board + Changelog** | Public roadmap voting and release log. See §6. |

| **NOT IN — do not market, do not mock up, do not imply** | |
|---|---|
| **Website CMS / site builder** | In development. Ships during the beta period. Founding Hosts will be first to use it and first to shape it. |

### 1.1 What this changes

**Priority 1 is temporarily gone.** The brand strategy names the 10-minute beautiful website as *the word-of-mouth trigger* — the thing that visibly changes Elsa's professional standing. Message Pillar 3 ("Website. Directory. Booking system. One subscription.") **cannot be used in beta marketing.** Neither can Problem 3. Lead with Pillars 1 and 2, plus a new beta-only pillar below.

**The honesty rule is absolute.** Brand character: *does not sell features not yet built.* No website mockups in the partner pack. No screenshots of a builder that doesn't exist. If a group owner posts a fake site builder screenshot, it is their credibility you've spent, not yours — and the SA host community is small enough that it comes back.

**The beta ICP shifts.** Without the website, the hosts who get most value on day one are those who **already have some web presence** and are drowning in OTA commission and admin. That is the upper end of Elsa, plus Pieter, plus Thandi. Brief partners explicitly: *"post this at the hosts in your group who already have a website but are losing money to Booking.com."* If they recruit hosts with no online presence at all, activation collapses.

**R999 is the finished-product price, not the current-product price.** It includes the website CMS and every feature added to the tier thereafter, at no extra cost. This is confirmed and it resolves the earlier concern — the question is no longer whether R999 is defensible without the website, but whether R999 is right for the full bundle. **Still worth verifying against current SA competitor pricing before it goes on the public site.**

It also means R999 can be live and transactable today without dishonesty, provided the Build Board and changelog are doing their job: a non-referred host paying R999 now can see exactly what's in progress and knows they get it free when it ships. The public roadmap is what makes selling a finished-product price on an unfinished product legitimate rather than sharp.

### 1.2 The scope gap turned into a campaign beat

The website builder shipping mid-beta is not an apology. It is a marketing moment:

> *"The website builder goes live this month. Every Founding Host gets it first — and it was built from 340 votes on the Build Board."*

That lands in month 2 or 3, exactly when an 8-month competition normally goes quiet. Plan for it as a scheduled asset drop to all partners.

### 2.2 Why Looking For leads

Every other feature in the beta is tooling — it saves the host time, organises their admin, and requires them to do the work. **Looking For is the only feature that puts money in a host's pocket in week one without them doing anything.**

Against the brand's own test — *every feature must save Elsa time or put money in Pieter's pocket* — it is the only one that reaches into the pocket.

## 3. PROGRAMME SHAPE

### 3.1 No cap. Rolling enrolment. Individual clocks.

Intake is **uncapped**. A partner can refer a thousand hosts and there is no ceiling on what they earn or score.

Urgency comes from two mechanics that scale infinitely:

**A. The individual clock.** Every host's four months starts **the day they join**. Their Decision Window is week 15 of *their own* subscription, not of the programme. Unlimited intake, and every single host still hits a real deadline with a real consequence. This is the highest-leverage mechanic in the programme — a deadline with a consequence is what converts a free user, and this one is personal to each host.

**B. Beta-exit scarcity.** *"Founding pricing exists while Wielo is in beta. When we exit beta it's gone — with 30 days' notice."* No cap, no invented date, completely honest, and it hands partners a genuine marketing moment when you eventually announce the exit. This replaces the artificial countdown entirely.

### 3.2 The host journey

| Week | What happens |
|---|---|
| 0 | Signs up via partner link. No card. Free guest account or host trial — see §6.4. |
| 1 | Attends a **Build Night** (weekly group session, 45 min) and gets live: property loaded, rooms, rates, calendar, directory listing published. |
| 1–2 | Directory listing live. Looking For alerts switched on. First quote sent. |
| 4, 8, 12 | Monthly feedback form. 10 minutes. Private, structured. |
| 4–16 | Build Board voting and submissions. Public. |
| 12 | Honest review request — **unconditional**, not tied to price. See §5.4. |
| 15–17 | **Decision Window.** Three weeks. Founding price or list price. |
| 17+ | Paying host. Partner starts earning commission. |

### 3.3 The competition timeline

The competition runs **8 calendar months** on a single continuous leaderboard. Because enrolment is rolling and clocks are individual, there is **no stage split** — activation points and conversion points are both earnable at any time. Monthly prizes provide the rhythm that stages used to.

Hosts who join in month 7 still count for activation points during the competition; their conversion lands after it closes and still pays commission. Nothing is wasted.


### 3.4 What "leaving beta" means — DECIDED

Founding pricing ends when Wielo exits beta, so this needs a written definition before it is ever said publicly. **All of the following, not any of them:**

| Gate | |
|---|---|
| **Time** | 8 months from beta start. Hard floor. |
| **The website CMS has shipped** | You cannot leave beta with the flagship feature unbuilt. This is the one nobody thinks to write down and the one that matters most. |
| **Reviews** | A meaningful body of honest, unincentivised public reviews. Set the number now — 25 is a defensible target against 300 activated hosts. |
| **Paying subscriptions** | At least 10 active paid subscriptions. |
| **Marketplace health** | 2-quotes-in-24h holding above 70% in every region you're marketing in. |

**A note on the 10-subscription gate.** Against a target of 135 paying hosts it is a floor rather than an ambition, and the 8-month time gate is what will actually bind. That's fine — but be aware that 10 is the number you'd hit and still be disappointed. The CMS gate and the marketplace-health gate are the ones doing real work here, because they're the two things that would genuinely embarrass you if you exited beta without them.

**30 days' notice to every Founding Host and every partner before the door closes.** That notice period is itself a marketing moment — the last honest push at Founding pricing, and a partner's best week of the whole programme.

## 4. OFFER ONE — THE FOUNDING PARTNER (GROUP OWNER)

### 4.1 Positioning

Do not approach them as affiliates. Approach them as the person who brings their community something valuable. The brand strategy is explicit: Elsa buys from peer recommendation, not advertising. The group owner's currency is being the one who knew first. Sell them that, then mention the money.

> *"You built a community of South African hosts who trust what you put in front of them. We built the platform they've been asking for. We'd like you to be the one who brings it to them — and to earn from it for as long as they stay."*

### 4.2 Cap the partners, not the hosts

**25 Founding Partners. Decided.** If 60 group owners compete, nobody tries — the leaderboard looks unwinnable by week three. Scarcity also makes the invitation feel like selection rather than a mass email, which matters for how they present it to their groups.

Hosts are uncapped. Partners are not. These are different decisions and the reasons are different.

### 4.3 What partners get

| | |
|---|---|
| **Lifetime recurring commission** | Every host they refer earns them money every month that host pays. No expiry, no cap, no reset. |
| **A rate that climbs** | 10% to 25%, set by their own monthly revenue. See §4.4. |
| **Conversion bonus** | R250 once-off per beta host who converts to a paid monthly plan. R400 for annual. Flat cash, paid immediately — this is what carries partners through the unpaid beta months. |
| **Co-branded landing page** | `wielo.co.za/partners/[their-group]` — their name, their group, their photo, their words at the top. Not a tracking link with a query string. A page that makes them look like the source. |
| **Done-for-you marketing pack** | Post copy, images, commission-maths graphic, video, comment reply bank, FAQ. They post; they don't write. Refreshed monthly. |
| **Their own Wielo account** | Free for life if they run a property. If not, a free account to gift to a member of their choosing. |
| **Founding Partner badge + named on wielo.co.za** | Public credit on the site. Status is the point. |
| **Roadmap input** | Monthly partner call. Their requests go onto the Build Board flagged as partner-submitted. |
| **Private partner group** | WhatsApp. Direct line to you. Not a support ticket. |
| **Live dashboard** | Referrals, activations, earnings, leaderboard position. Updated daily. Nothing kills an affiliate programme faster than not being able to see the score. |

### 4.4 The commission ladder — DECIDED

**One rate, set by performance, recalculated monthly.** No 12-month split, no expiry dates, no per-host tracking. The existing affiliate system already supports revenue-banded rates, so this is a configuration, not a build.

| Their monthly revenue from referred hosts | Rate | Roughly, in hosts |
|---|---|---|
| Up to R10,000 | **10%** | 1–16 |
| R10,000 – R25,000 | **15%** | 17–41 |
| R25,000 – R50,000 | **20%** | 42–83 |
| R50,000+ | **25%** | 84+ |

**Two mechanics that make it work:**

**The rate applies to their entire book, not just revenue above the threshold.** Cross R25,000 and *everything* moves to 20%, backdated to that month. The retroactive jump is the whole motivational engine — marginal-rate ladders don't pull, because the next rand earns more but the existing book doesn't move.

**It recalculates monthly on revenue actually collected.** Which means a rate goes *down* if their hosts churn. This is the quiet strength of the ladder over any fixed rate: it makes partners care about whether the hosts they referred stay. Nobody has to be asked to care about retention; the structure does it.

*Note on the original thresholds proposed (R50k / R150k):* those are 83 and 250 hosts respectively. Almost every partner would have sat at 10% permanently, looking at a ladder they could never climb. The bands above put the first two rungs within reach of a normal good month, which is what makes a ladder motivating rather than decorative.

### 4.5 The earnings maths to put in front of them

| Hosts converted | Monthly revenue | Rate | **Their monthly** | Their year 1 incl. bonuses |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 10 | R5,990 | 10% | **R599** | R9,688 |
| 20 | R11,980 | 15% | **R1,797** | R26,564 |
| 40 | R23,960 | 15% | **R3,594** | R53,128 |
| 50 | R29,950 | 20% | **R5,990** | R81,880 |
| 84 | R50,316 | 25% | **R12,579** | R172,948 |
| 150 | R89,850 | 25% | **R22,463** | R306,050 |

*Based on R599/month Founding hosts. Annual-plan hosts pay R499/month and earn proportionally; the year is collected upfront but commission pays monthly against it (§13.15).*

**The line that sells it:** *"Fifty hosts from your group is about R6,000 a month, every month, for work you did once. Get to eighty-four and your whole book moves to 25% — that's over R12,000 a month. There's no ceiling and no expiry."*

**The honest trade-off to be aware of internally:** this pays a small partner less than a flat 25% would. The conversion bonuses (R250/R400 per converting host, paid immediately) are what carry them through the early months, and the ladder is a better story than a fixed rate that can only ever go down.

### 4.6 What we ask

- **Minimum 4 posts** across the beta phase (we supply all of them).
- One **pinned post** for the first 30 days.
- **Honest representation** — no promising features that don't exist. Specifically: **no website builder claims.**
- Attend at least 2 of the monthly partner calls.


### 4.7 The partner dashboard — potential earnings

**The problem this solves.** Partners earn no commission for four months. Hosts pay nothing during beta, so until conversions land in month five the only cash is the conversion bonuses. A dashboard reading R0.00 for sixteen weeks is actively demoralising, and month three is exactly when partners go quiet.

**The rule that keeps it safe: build a calculator, not a forecast.**

A forecast says *"you will earn R4,200."* A calculator says *"if all 40 of your live hosts convert at R599, that's R3,594 a month."* The second is arithmetic — it cannot be wrong, and nobody feels misled when reality lands lower, because you never claimed it wouldn't. Show a projection, miss it, and a partner concludes the whole programme was oversold and says so in a group of four thousand hosts.

**What goes on the dashboard, in this order:**

| Element | Example | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Earned so far** | R2,750 | Actual rands, top of page. Conversion bonuses during beta. Never hide the real number behind the hypothetical one. |
| **Live listings · score · rank** | 40 · 40 pts · 3rd | Facts. |
| **Your book at full conversion** | *"If every live host converts at R599: **R3,594/month**"* | Stated as a condition, with a plain note that nobody converts 100%. |
| **Over 12 months** | *"**R43,128**"* | Same conditional. This is where the evergreen story stops being abstract — it's the number that makes them post again. |
| **Distance to your next rate** | *"6 more listings takes you to 15%"* | Probably the most motivating element on the page, and it's a fact rather than a projection. |

**From month five, replace the ceiling with reality.** Once real conversion data exists, swap "if every host converts" for the actual programme-wide rate: *"At the current 47% conversion rate across all partners, your book is worth **R1,689/month**."* Honest, grounded in real data, and self-correcting.

**Two guardrails:**

- **Use the word "potential."** Never "pending," never "estimated earnings" — pending implies owed, and a partner who believes they're owed R43,000 is a problem you cannot talk your way out of.
- **The §4.5 earnings table is itself an earnings claim.** Label it illustrative and state the assumptions on the same page. The Consumer Protection Act takes a dim view of unqualified income representations, and this is trivial to get right up front and expensive to fix later.

## 5. OFFER TWO — THE FOUNDING HOST

### 5.1 The offer, stated plainly

> **Four months of Wielo. No charge.**
> Full booking management. Curated directory listing. Looking For — guests actively searching, delivered to you. Every guest's full contact details. Zero commission on every booking.
>
> **You're joining a beta.** That means some rough edges, and it means your feedback actually changes what gets built. The website builder is in development and lands during your four months — you'll be first to use it and first to shape it.
>
> **When your four months end, your price is R599/month — locked, for as long as you stay.** R499/month annual. Everyone else pays R999.
>
> **And your price never moves while the product keeps growing.** The website builder, and everything else we add to your plan, is included at no extra cost. Forever. You pay R599 for a product that will be worth far more than R999 by next year.
>
> **Founding pricing exists while Wielo is in beta. When we leave beta, it goes.**

### 5.2 Why this works on the beta ICP

- **"What if it doesn't work for me?"** → Four months to find out. Nothing to lose.
- **"I'm not technical."** → Build Night. We set it up with you, live, in 45 minutes.
- **"Who is Wielo?"** → The person who runs your group is vouching for us.
- **"Can I afford it?"** → You already pay Booking.com more than this every month. Here's the number.
- **"Why is it cheaper for me?"** → Because you were here when it was rough, and you helped fix it.

### 5.3 The full package

| | |
|---|---|
| **4 months, full access** | Everything currently built. No tier restrictions. Value: R3,996. |
| **Founding price locked** | R599/month or R499/month annual. Never increases while they remain subscribed. |
| **Every future feature on their plan, included** | The website CMS and everything else added to their tier arrives at no extra cost, permanently. The gap between what they pay and what they get widens every year. This is the single strongest line in the Founding Host offer — **"your price never goes up, and the product never stops growing."** |
| **Build Night** | Weekly 45-minute group session — hosts get live on the call. Recorded. Runs every week. 1:1 sessions reserved for multi-property hosts (Thandi). Scales to any number and removes the single biggest churn risk in the research: no result in 30 days. |
| **The Founding Host group** | Private group for beta hosts only. Not a perk — infrastructure. Deflects support load onto peers, generates feedback without being asked, turns hosts into each other's social proof. This is where the brand strategy's flywheel actually starts. |
| **Founding Host badge** | Permanent badge on their directory listing. Permanent priority ranking in directory search. |
| **Build Board weight** | Founding Host votes carry visible weight and their requests are tagged as such. |
| **Their story featured** | Case study, social post, homepage. Free marketing for their property. |
| **First-booking guarantee** | No direct booking in 90 days? We work with them until there is one — via the 90-Day Direct Booking Playbook plus a monthly group clinic, with 1:1 rescue for hosts who complete the playbook and still have nothing. Real promise, finite hours. |

### 5.4 The commitment — and the review correction

**This changed from v1.0 and the change matters.**

v1.0 tied keeping the R599 price to publishing a review, and awarded partners points for reviews. That is an incentivised review. Google strips them, Facebook can too, and the ASA code requires the material connection to be disclosed. Reviews you can't keep are worth nothing, and reviews pulled at month six leave a hole where your social proof was.

**Corrected structure:**

| | |
|---|---|
| **Tied to the Founding price** | Publish your listing within 14 days. Complete the monthly feedback form (4 × 10 minutes). Report what breaks. **This is a service exchange, and it's clean.** |
| **NOT tied to anything** | The review. Asked unconditionally at month 3, when they've had a result. Honest, not glowing — if it's bad, we'd rather know. Ask them to mention they were a beta tester; it reads as more credible, not less. |
| **Partner scoring** | Points for *"host completed feedback,"* never for *"host published a review."* |

### 5.5 No card at signup

**No payment card required to join.** It maximises volume and removes Elsa's last hesitation. The trade-off is a harder conversion at the Decision Window — which is precisely why the individual clock, the Build Night, the first-booking guarantee, the monthly contact, and the Build Board all exist. Those five things, not a card on file, are what convert her.


### 5.6 What R599 actually buys — DECIDED, with one problem to solve

**There is one plan.** Not One / Pro / Agency. One product, R999 list, R599 Founding, R499 Founding annual.

**Included, permanently, on the Founding lock:**

One business · one listing · unlimited rooms within that listing · unlimited bookings · unlimited guest records · full inbox · add-ons · specials · coupons · Looking For · directory listing · and one website when the CMS ships. Plus everything added to this plan thereafter, at no extra cost.

**The boundary of the lifetime lock — write this sentence into the Founding Host terms:**

> *Your Founding price covers the Wielo plan as it stands and every feature added to it. Additional listings, and any separately priced product we may launch, are not included.*

That single sentence is what stops "everything forever" becoming a liability in year three. It costs nothing to say now and cannot be added later.

**The gap: Thandi doesn't fit the pricing.**

**Wielo already supports multiple listings under one host account. Nothing needs building on the product side.** What's missing is purely commercial: a price for additional listings and the rules that enforce it.

One business, one listing does not serve a property manager with 15 separate properties — and the master brand strategy names multi-property managers as the highest-leverage segment, while §8.3 makes them the single biggest prize on the leaderboard (15 listings, 15 points). Without a price, partners are being pointed at customers you can't sell to.

**Two implementation routes:**

| Route | |
|---|---|
| **Add-on rule on the existing plan** *(recommended)* | One plan, one subscription, a per-listing line item that scales. Simplest to explain, simplest to bill, and it keeps "there is one Wielo plan" true — which matters, because the moment you have tiers you have upgrade conversations and feature-gating decisions you don't want yet. |
| **A separate multi-property tier** | Cleaner if you later want genuinely different features for managers — bulk actions, owner statements, per-owner reporting. Worth revisiting when Thandi asks for those, not before. |

**Proposed pricing:**

| | List | Founding |
|---|---|---|
| First listing | R999 | R599 |
| Each additional listing | R299 | R179 |

A 15-property manager pays R599 + (14 × R179) = **R3,105/month.** One account, one invoice, one relationship.

**Why the economics work.** She pays roughly five times a single host and is worth more than five to you: one support thread instead of fifteen, near-zero churn because migrating fifteen properties is genuinely painful, and fifteen properties onto the directory in one afternoon.

**The Founding lock applies to additional listings at the same locked rate.** State this explicitly in the terms, or a manager who adds a sixteenth property in year two will reasonably expect R179 and be told R299.

## 6. THE BUILD BOARD + CHANGELOG

*New in v2.0. This is what makes "beta" structurally true rather than merely claimed.*

### 6.1 What it is

**The Build Board** — a public page where anyone can submit a feature request, a bug, or an improvement, and anyone can vote. Sorted by votes. Filterable by status.

**Shipped** — a public changelog of every release, in plain language, **crediting the host who asked for it by name and property.**

Both publicly viewable without an account. **A free Wielo account is required to submit or vote.**

### 6.2 Why it earns its build cost

1. **It proves the beta is real.** Anyone can see a request, its vote count, and its ship date. The credibility risk in a marketing-flavoured "beta" disappears.
2. **It's a lead magnet disguised as a product page.** Every host annoyed by NightsBridge or Booking.com who posts about it hands you an email address and a stated pain.
3. **It gives partners renewable content.** *"Marie from our group asked for split-payment deposits. 47 votes. Wielo shipped it Tuesday."* A group owner posting about their own member. You cannot buy that.
4. **It's free prioritisation research** — answering the roadmap questions the master strategy leaves open.
5. **It's a public archive of competitor gaps.** Hosts will post what they wish existed. Read the board as market research.
6. **It makes the missing website builder an asset.** *"Website builder — In progress · 340 votes · shipping during beta."* Honest, visible, and evidence you listen.
7. **SEO.** Every request is an indexable page carrying real host language about real SA accommodation problems.

### 6.3 Governance — this is what makes or breaks it

**Five statuses:**

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Under review** | Received. Being read. |
| **Planned** | Committed. No date promised. |
| **In progress** | Being built now. |
| **Shipped** | Live. Links to the changelog entry. |
| **Not doing** | Declined — **with a stated reason.** |

**"Not doing" is the trust-builder.** A board where nothing is ever declined reads as theatre within two months. Declining honestly, in public, with an explanation, is exactly the brand's *tells the truth even when uncomfortable.*

**State on the page, plainly:** votes inform the roadmap; they do not decide it. Keep decision rights and say so. Hosts respect that more than a fake democracy.

**Non-negotiables:**
- **Seed it before launch.** 20–30 real items spread across all five statuses. An empty board looks dead.
- **Every request gets a response within 7 days.** Even if the response is "under review."
- **Ship something visible every two weeks** during beta, minimum. A board of items that never move is worse than no board.
- **Never let it replace the private feedback form.** The board gets loud requests; the form gets uncomfortable truths nobody posts publicly. Different jobs. Keep both.

### 6.4 The Wielo account — DECIDED

**Confirmed: one account.** The same free Wielo account posts a Looking For request, submits to the Build Board, and votes. One signup does lead capture, demand generation, and roadmap voting.

**DECIDED — UI rename only.** The system keeps `guest` as the internal role; every user-facing string changes. Screens to update: signup (`/signup/guest`), the dashboard header, nav, emails, and the Looking For page. Also rename the destination — "Guest dashboard" becomes "Your Wielo account."

**Call it a Wielo account, not a "guest account."** Free, for everyone. Hosts have a Wielo account *with a subscription*. This matters for two reasons: the free tier isn't guest-only so roadmap voting makes sense on it, and a guest who happens to own a guesthouse converts with one click and no second signup. **Ask "Do you own accommodation?" at signup** — that single field turns the guest funnel into a host lead source.

| Tier | Who | Can |
|---|---|---|
| **Wielo account (free)** | Anyone | Post a Looking For request · Submit to the Build Board · Vote · Save directory listings |
| **Beta host** | Referred by a partner | Everything above + full booking management + directory listing + Looking For responses. 4 months, no charge. |
| **Paid host** | Post-Decision Window | Everything, at R599 / R499 / R999 |

**Three consequences to build for:**

**Segment the votes.** Free-tier guests will outnumber hosts by a wide margin. A guest voting on host booking features is noise. Tag every account by role. Host votes visible and weighted; guest votes surfaced on guest-facing items. Without this the Build Board's signal degrades within two months.

**Mind the masking optics.** Guests posting a Looking For request hand travel dates and budget to strangers, so contact details should stay masked until the guest accepts a quote. That is defensible — guest privacy, not host lock-in — but Wielo's entire brand is built on *"Booking.com masks your data."* Write the explanatory language before launch, or someone hands you that contradiction in a Facebook comment.

**Free accounts become a public growth number.** "14,000 travellers on Wielo" is what makes a host want a directory listing. Publish it once it's credible.

### 6.5 The two-sided problem — the biggest risk this creates

**Looking For is a marketplace, and the partner programme only fills one side of it.**

Accommodation Facebook groups are *host* groups. Every Founding Partner brings supply. None bring demand. If a host switches on Looking For alerts in week one and finds eleven requests nationally, none in their region, you have led with the feature that fails first — worse than not marketing it at all.

**Four demand sources, in order of weight:**

| Source | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Guest-side group partners** | SA travel and "where to stay" groups are enormous. **Do not build a second scheme — see §6.5a.** |
| **Meta ads to guests** | Cheap on the guest side. *"Tell us what you're looking for. South African hosts come to you."* This is marketplace liquidity spend, not optional marketing support. Budget from day one. |
| **Hosts' own guest lists** | Every beta host has past guests. A prompt to invite them converts supply into demand at zero cost. |
| **Directory SEO** | Slowest, compounds hardest, meaningful from roughly month four. |

**The guest-side conversion surface for all of this is the Looking For page — see §7, which specifies the flow that has to be built before any of this spend makes sense.**

**Tactical rule regardless of the above: do not let partners lead with Looking For in month one.** Sequence the marketing pack — commission maths and guest data first; Looking For introduced around week 4–6, once there is real request volume to screenshot. Hold marketing asset #4 back until liquidity exists.



### 6.5a Guest-side partners — no second scheme

The instinct to avoid complexity is right. A separate compensation model means a second dashboard, a second set of terms, a second fraud surface, and a second thing to explain. Don't.

**Default: give travel-group owners the same Founding Partner deal.** One system, one dashboard, one agreement. Most SA travel group owners either know hosts personally or have hosts inside their groups, so the standard commission ladder gives them a real earning path. And their incentive to drive guest demand is structural rather than paid: more guests posting means more hosts want to be on Wielo, which grows the book they earn from.

**Pair it with something free that they value more than money: the regional digest.** Every week, send each partner a shareable summary of live requests in their region — *"14 people are looking for places in the Berg this month, R1,500–R3,000 a night."* Group owners are perpetually short of good content. This is genuinely useful to post, it drives both sides at once, it costs you a scheduled query, and it makes the partner look plugged in. Expect this to outperform any cash incentive you could design.

**Exception: two or three large, genuinely host-free travel groups.** For these, a flat **R1,000–R1,500 per month retainer** against an agreed posting cadence, paid from the R40,000 guest-demand budget. Fixed, predictable, no tracking, cancellable monthly. Justify it the way you'd justify any media buy — if it delivers reach cheaper than Meta does, it's a good buy.

**Never do per-request bounties.** Paying per published request invites junk at exactly the volume where policing it costs more than the requests are worth, and a board full of fake requests destroys host trust faster than an empty one.

### 6.6 The guest demand budget — R40,000 over 8 months

**R5,000 per month, committed. This is marketplace liquidity spend, not marketing spend** — it is the cost of the demand side existing at all, and it should be treated as infrastructure in the budget, not as something to trim in a tight month.

**Within each month:**

| Split | Amount | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Prospecting | R3,500 | Cold, region-targeted, driving to `/looking-for/start` |
| Retargeting | R1,000 | People who started a request and didn't publish; directory browsers |
| Testing | R500 | New creative and new regions. Never skip this — it's what finds the next winner |

**Across the eight months:**

| Months | Regions | Gate to advance |
|---|---|---|
| **1–2** | **One region only.** Recommend the Drakensberg — it is close to you, host recruitment is easier, and the seasonal pull is strong. | 2-quotes-in-24h above 70% |
| **3–4** | Add a second region | Same gate, held in both |
| **5–6** | Three regions + retargeting scaled | Cost per published request holding |
| **7–8** | Scale the winners only. Kill anything underperforming rather than spreading thinner. | — |

**Numbers to manage against.** These are thresholds for decision-making, not published benchmarks — establish your own baseline in month one and manage against the trend:

| Metric | Good | Investigate |
|---|---|---|
| Landing page → step 1 started | Above 50% | Below 35% — the page above the fold is the problem |
| Step 1 started → request published | Above 40% | Below 25% — a specific step is losing them; the instrumentation in §7.4 tells you which |
| Cost per published request | Under R40 | Over R80 — creative or targeting, in that order |
| Published → 2+ quotes in 24h | Above 70% | Below 40% — **stop spending**, see below |

**The hold rule, and it is absolute:** if 2-quotes-in-24h drops below 40% in a region, **pause spend in that region and move the money to host recruitment there.** More demand into a market that cannot answer it burns guests and hosts at the same time, and guests who get no reply do not come back.

**Seasonality.** SA domestic travel peaks around December–January, Easter, and school holidays, and guests post requests roughly four to eight weeks ahead. If the flat R5k can flex, weight it toward the six weeks preceding a holiday period rather than spreading evenly — same R40,000, materially more requests.

**Two free channels to run alongside, which may well outperform the spend:** post the live board manually into guest-side travel groups in your own voice, and prompt every beta host to invite their past guests. A guesthouse with eleven years of bookings has a list, and those people already trust them.

### 6.7 Sequencing

**Changelog first.** Nearly trivial to build and it starts working immediately if every entry credits the host by name and property. Live at beta launch.

**Build Board second.** Live within the first four weeks of beta. It needs the seed content and a moderation process more than it needs code.

### 6.8 Minor risk, noted and dismissed

A public board reveals roadmap to competitors. At this stage, speed and trust matter far more than secrecy, and the incumbent is not moving fast. Proceed.

## 7. THE LOOKING FOR FUNNEL — PILLAR GROWTH PAGE

*New in v2.1. This is the highest-ROI small build in the entire plan.*

### 7.1 The problem with the page as it stands

`wielo.co.za/looking-for/start` is a good sales page. It has live example requests, a clear three-step explainer, and honest benefit copy. Every single call to action on it points to `/signup/guest?next=/portal/looking-for/new`.

That is registration-first. You are asking a stranger to create an account before they have experienced anything, in exchange for a promise. It is the highest-friction possible ordering of the same two actions, and it is why the page will underperform whatever traffic you send it.

**The fix costs nothing in features and roughly doubles completions: let them do the thing first, and create the account as a byproduct of finishing it.**

By the time someone has typed where they're going, when, who's coming, what they can spend and what matters to them, they have invested five minutes. They are not abandoning at the last step. Ask for the account *there*, framed as a service question — *"Where should hosts send your offers?"* — and it stops being registration at all.

### 7.2 Why this is a pillar page, strategically

| | |
|---|---|
| **It is the only guest-side conversion surface Wielo has** | Every other page on the site sells to hosts. This one turns a member of the public into a Wielo account holder. |
| **It de-risks the biggest hole in the plan** | §6.5 — Looking For is a two-sided marketplace and the partner programme only fills the supply side. This page *is* the demand side. |
| **Every published request is an indexable page** | "Family self-catering Champagne Valley August" is a real long-tail search. A thousand live requests is a thousand pages of genuine, unfakeable SA travel language. This compounds and no competitor has it. |
| **It is the landing page for the R5k regional ad test** | And for every rand of guest-side spend after it. |
| **Every request is host marketing** | *"14 people are looking for a place in the Berg this month. Are you seeing any of them?"* That screenshot sells Wielo to hosts better than any feature list. |
| **The free account it creates does three jobs** | Looking For, Build Board voting, saved listings — per §6.4. One signup, three functions. |

### 7.3 The flow — step by step

**Principle: order the steps by momentum. Easiest and most engaging first, commitment last.**

| Step | Ask | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Where are you going?** | Typeahead on SA regions and towns. **Include an "I'm not sure yet — open to suggestions" option.** Every OTA forces a destination; this one doesn't. That option captures the undecided traveller — the highest-value guest a host can pitch to — and no competitor can match it. |
| **2** | **When?** | Date picker with "my dates are flexible" and "not sure yet, roughly [month]". Don't force precision that kills completion. |
| **3** | **Who's coming?** | Adults, children with ages, pets. Quick steppers, no typing. |
| **4** | **What's your budget?** | Range slider. Toggle per-night / total. Show them the implied other figure live so they don't have to do the maths. |
| **5** | **What matters to you?** | Short free-text box, plus quick-select chips: self-catering · pet-friendly · braai facilities · pool · hiking from the door · wheelchair access · load-shedding backup · secure parking · walking distance to the beach. Chips do most of the work; the text box catches the rest. |
| **6** | **Preview** | **Their request rendered as the exact card hosts will see.** This is the commitment device — seeing your own post as a real object creates ownership and makes step 7 feel like completing something rather than starting something. Do not skip this step. |
| **7** | **"Where should hosts send your offers?"** | Name + email. Account created silently. **Magic link, no password.** Redirect straight to the guest dashboard with the live request on it. |

### 7.4 Build rules that matter more than the design

**Passwordless.** Email + magic link. Every password field costs completions, and a guest who uses Wielo twice a year will forget a password and never return. This is not a nice-to-have.

**Nothing gated until step 7.** No account wall, no "sign in to continue," no modal. If a person can't reach the preview without an account, the whole change is wasted.

**Persist progress locally.** Someone who gets to step 4 and closes the tab should resume where they left off. Note the honest trade-off: post-first means you have no email address for abandoners, so you cannot email-recover them. Retargeting pixel plus local resume is the compensation. It's worth it — capturing an email early is precisely the friction being removed.

**Instrument every step.** You cannot improve this page without knowing which step loses people. Track: page view → step 1 started → each step completed → preview reached → account created → request published.

**Public and indexed by default, with a private option.** The board at `/looking-for` should be crawlable. Per POPIA, publish first name only, region rather than exact address, and never the email. The existing "keep it public or private, runs 30 days" control is right — keep it.

### 7.5 The half of the build that isn't on this page

**A request with no quote is worse than no request at all.** A guest who posts, waits three days and hears nothing does not come back, and tells people. Building the guest flow without the host response loop actively damages the brand.

Ship these at the same time:

| | |
|---|---|
| **Host alerts within minutes** | **Email at launch. WhatsApp in phase 2 — see §7.6.** A host who finds out about a matching request 18 hours later has already lost the guest, so the email has to be instant, mobile-first, and one tap from the alert to the quote screen. |
| **Quote in under two minutes** | From alert to submitted offer, on a phone, without opening a laptop. Pre-filled price from their rates, one text box, send. |
| **Regional matching** | Alert only hosts who can plausibly serve the request. Blanket alerts train hosts to ignore them, and once ignored they stay ignored. |
| **Quote-count visible to the guest** | "3 hosts are preparing offers" while they wait. Manages expectation and stops the silence feeling like failure. |


### 7.6 WhatsApp alerts — the sequencing and the mechanics

**First, the strategic point: don't build it yet.**

WhatsApp is the right channel for this market, but you don't know what it's worth until email tells you. Ship email alerts, then measure **alert → quote submitted**. If email converts at 60%, WhatsApp is a worthwhile uplift on an already-working loop. If it converts at 15%, the problem is the quote screen, not the channel — and WhatsApp would only deliver more hosts to a broken step, faster. Fix what the number tells you to fix.

**What email has to do to earn that read:** sent within 60 seconds of the request publishing, readable on a phone without pinching, subject line carrying the region and dates, and a single button that opens a pre-filled quote screen. If a host has to log in, navigate and search, the channel isn't the problem.

**Then, the mechanics, so it's planned into the codebase now rather than retrofitted.**

| | |
|---|---|
| **Route** | **Meta WhatsApp Cloud API, direct.** Skip a BSP (Twilio, Bird, 360dialog). BSPs add per-message markup for convenience you don't need — you have developers, and the Cloud API is well documented. |
| **The constraint that shapes everything** | You cannot send arbitrary outbound WhatsApp messages. Business-initiated messages must use **pre-approved templates**, and approval takes time. Design around templates from day one; don't discover this late. |
| **The 24-hour window** | Once a host replies to you, you can message freely for 24 hours. This is what eventually enables a conversational quote flow — host replies "1800" to the alert and it's submitted. Phase 3, but design the templates so it's possible. |
| **Start with one template** | *"New stay request in {{region}}, {{dates}}, {{guests}} guests, up to {{budget}}. Tap to quote: {{link}}"* — one template, one job. Add "guest replied to your quote" and "your request has no quotes yet" later. |
| **Opt-in is mandatory** | Both Meta's policy and POPIA require explicit consent. A checkbox at host onboarding: *"Send me WhatsApp alerts when a guest is looking for a place like mine."* Store the timestamp and the wording consented to. |
| **Cost** | Meta charges per conversation/message and the rates have changed more than once. **Check current SA rates before budgeting** — don't model on anything older than a few months. |

**Recommended sequencing:** email alerts at beta launch → measure for 4–6 weeks → WhatsApp templates submitted for approval in month 2 → live month 3. SMS is not worth building; low engagement and it costs more than email to do badly.

### 7.7 Passwordless — DECIDED

Magic link at account creation. **No password field anywhere in the Looking For flow.** Guests can set a password later from account settings if they want one; most never will and won't need to. Every returning visit is an emailed link.

Two things to get right: the link should be long-lived enough to survive a guest checking email on a different device (24 hours is reasonable), and single-use. And the email that carries it is now a critical delivery path — get SPF, DKIM and DMARC right before launch, because a magic link in spam is a lost account with no recovery route.

### 7.8 POPIA on public request pages — DECIDED

Requests are public and indexed by default, with a private option. To keep that POPIA-safe:

| Publish | Never publish |
|---|---|
| First name only | Surname |
| Region and town | Exact address or suburb-level precision |
| Dates, guest count, budget band | Email, phone, or any contact detail |
| The free-text description | Anything the guest didn't knowingly write for public display |

Plus: explicit consent at the point of posting with the public/private choice shown *before* submit, not buried in terms; 30-day auto-expiry (already built); one-click delete that also de-indexes; and a plain-language line explaining that hosts see the request and can send offers, but get no contact details until the guest accepts a quote.

**For the attorney:** Wielo and the hosts are likely separate responsible parties once a guest accepts a quote and data passes across. Confirm where that transfer sits and what has to be disclosed.

### 7.9 The metric that decides everything

Not signups. Not requests posted. This one:

> **Percentage of published requests receiving at least two quotes within 24 hours.**

| Reading | What it means | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| **Above 70%** | The marketplace works. | Scale guest-side ad spend hard and immediately. |
| **40–70%** | Working, thin in places. | Recruit hosts in the underserved regions before spending more on demand. |
| **Below 40%** | Host density is too low. | **Stop guest ad spend.** More demand into a market that can't answer it burns guests and hosts simultaneously. Fix supply first. |

### 7.10 Build priority

Against the other builds competing for the same developer time:

**DECIDED: the Build Board and changelog ship *before* beta launch, not during it.** The reasoning is right — from day one the team should be running marketing and sales, not firefighting a half-built feedback loop. It pushes the launch date out, and that is the correct trade.

**Everything below is pre-launch. Nothing starts until all four are live:**

1. **Looking For public flow (§7.3) + host quote loop (§7.5)** — highest priority. De-risks the largest hole in the plan and is the landing page for R40,000 of ad spend.
2. **Changelog** — `/change-log` already exists. Populate it, backfill recent releases, start crediting hosts by name.
3. **Build Board** — built, seeded with 20–30 items across all five statuses, moderation process agreed.
4. **Wielo account rename + magic link** — small, and both are in the critical path of the funnel.

**In parallel, not blocking launch:** the website CMS continues and ships mid-beta as a campaign beat (§1.2). WhatsApp alerts follow in month 2–3 per §7.6.

**Do not spend the R5k on ads until §7.3 and §7.5 are both live.** Sending paid traffic to the registration-first page as it stands wastes most of the budget and produces a misleading read on demand.

## 8. OFFER THREE — THE COMPETITION

### 8.1 Name and frame

**The Wielo Founding Race** — the race to bring South African hosts onto the platform.

No target number, no ceiling. Open-ended leaderboard, which is the right shape now that intake is uncapped: a partner in tenth place in month six still has reason to post, because there's no finish line they've been locked out of.

Frame it as a shared mission with a leaderboard, not a sales contest. Partners are *bringing their community in*, not selling.

### 8.2 Rules

**Who:** Invited Founding Partners only. Capped at 25.
**Duration:** 8 calendar months, single continuous leaderboard, no stage split.
**Winner:** Highest total points at the end of month 8.

### 8.3 Scoring — one line

**One point per active published listing from your referrals. That is the entire system.**

**Two rules:**

**1. Only listings that are currently live count.** A host who leaves stops counting from the next nightly recompute. No clawback windows, no 90-day timers, no disputes — the leaderboard is simply always true.

**2. Each listing counts separately.** A manager with 15 properties scores 15.

**The line partners will remember:** *"How many of your hosts are live right now? That's your score."*

### 8.3a Why activation, and why only activation

**Why the competition doesn't score conversion.** The commission ladder in §4.4 already pays partners on conversion — in cash, every month, permanently. Scoring it in the competition as well pays twice for one outcome, and it's the outcome partners control least. The two jobs separate cleanly:

> **The commission ladder rewards the result. The competition rewards the effort.**

**Why activation is fair to score, even though the host performs the action.** Publishing a listing looks like an internal host action, but what it measures is *who the partner chose to invite*. A partner who blasts their whole group gets roughly 15% activation. A partner who personally messages the thirty serious hosts they know gets 70%. Same link, same posting effort, completely different quality. Targeting judgement is the most valuable thing an affiliate does, and activation is the only number that captures it.

**Why not score signups instead.** In a free-beta model a signup costs nothing and proves nothing. Pay on signup and you get signups — two hundred curious clicks, a hundred and ninety of whom never return, and a leaderboard that rewards the partner who did the least useful work.

**What one-line scoring handles for free:**

| | |
|---|---|
| Churn | Host leaves, point disappears. Automatic. |
| Front-loading | Score is computed live at close on currently-active listings. 100 referrals in month one with 60 surviving scores 60. Nothing banks. |
| Multi-property hosts | 15 listings, 15 points. Thandi stays the biggest prize on the board. |
| Recruitment quality | A poorly targeted recruit never publishes and never scores. |
| Gaming | You cannot fake a listing with a verified address and photos. |
| Conversion | Still ferociously incentivised — in rands, monthly, forever, which is where partners actually feel it. |

**And the test that matters:** a group owner can hold their score in their head and explain it to someone else in one sentence. They will be explaining it, repeatedly, and any system needing a paragraph loses.

### 8.3b The runaway leader — and the fix

Front-loading is handled, but dominance isn't. If one partner genuinely brings 100 hosts who all stay, they are uncatchable by month three and the other 24 quietly stop posting. You'd have lost the programme to keep one performer.

**Fix: the monthly campaigns score net change within the window, not total.** Whoever adds the most live listings in July wins July. The field resets eight times, and a partner with 6 listings can beat one with 90. The dominant partner still wins the main race — correctly — while everyone else always has something winnable in front of them.

This is why the campaign engine needs a **scoring mode** setting (§8.7.3). It is the difference between concurrent campaigns being useful and being decorative.

**And the backstop:** the commission ladder runs underneath regardless of rank. No partner is ever playing for nothing.

### 8.3c This is a setting, not a commitment

The campaign engine holds point weights as configuration. Conversion weights exist in the schema and are simply set to zero for this campaign. If activation-only proves wrong in month three, it is a number change, not a rebuild.

### 8.4 Prizes

| | |
|---|---|
**Total pool: R40,000.** Allocation:

| | Cash | Permanent rate floor |
|---|---|---|
| **1st** | **R15,000** + "Wielo Founding Partner of the Year," named on the website | **20%** |
| **2nd** | **R7,000** | **15%** |
| **3rd** | **R3,000** | **12%** |

**The rate floor is the real prize.** With the ladder in §4.4, a partner sitting at 10% who wins first place doubles their rate permanently and still climbs above 20% as their book grows. It costs nothing on the day and compounds for years — which makes it worth more to them than the cash, and cheaper for you than a bigger cheque.

**Momentum prizes** — R15,000 of the pool, because an 8-month competition with one payout at the end goes quiet by month two:

| | |
|---|---|
| Monthly leaderboard | R1,000 to whoever adds the most live listings that month · **net change, not total** (§8.3b) · 8 × R1,000 = **R8,000** |
| First to 10 points | **R2,000**, once, whoever gets there first. *Note: a single 15-listing manager claims this outright — that's correct, they landed the most valuable referral in the programme.* |
| Fast Start | **R1,000** to *any* partner reaching 5 points in their first 30 days · budget 5 × R1,000 = **R5,000** · not competitive, everyone who hits it gets it |

**Total: R25,000 placings + R15,000 momentum = R40,000.** Against a realistic 135 paying hosts at blended R550, that is roughly six weeks of the resulting MRR.

### 8.5 Anti-gaming — non-negotiable

- One account per real, verifiable property. Address and photos verified before a host counts as qualified.
- No self-referral. No family accounts that don't operate as businesses.
- Commission clawed back on any refunded or charged-back payment. *(Points need no clawback rule — see §8.3 rule 1.)*
- No paid search advertising on "Wielo" or variants.
- Wielo verifies all activations. Wielo's decision on qualification is final.
- Fake accounts = forfeit of all points, all prizes, and all commission.

### 8.6 Legal

1. **Promotional competition rules.** Under the Consumer Protection Act, rules must be available free to every entrant and retained for three years. Publish at a fixed URL, link from every partner communication.
2. **Affiliate agreement.** Signed one-pager per partner: commission terms, clawback, POPIA obligations (they handle host contact data), termination, non-transferability if they sell the group.
3. **Review disclosure.** Because reviews are now unconditional, disclosure risk is largely removed — but ensure the ask never implies a benefit.

*Have a South African commercial attorney review all three. This document is not legal advice.*


### 8.7 The campaign engine — competitions as reusable infrastructure

**The principle: the system fixes what can be scored. The campaign configures everything else.** Partners learn the scoring model once and it never changes. What changes between campaigns is dates, eligibility, weights and prizes.

#### 8.7.1 Scoring events — system-level, fixed

Three events, and nothing else is ever scoreable. Keeping this list short is what keeps every campaign comprehensible.

| Event | Default weight |
|---|---|
| `listing_published` | **1** |
| `subscription_started_monthly` | 0 for this campaign — reserved in schema |
| `subscription_started_annual` | 0 for this campaign — reserved in schema |

#### 8.7.2 The architectural decision that makes rule 1 free

**Score is a live query against current state, not an accumulated ledger.**

Recompute nightly from the partner's *currently active* referred hosts. Don't write points to a running total when events fire.

This is what makes *"your score only counts hosts still with Wielo"* cost nothing to implement. There is no clawback job, no reversal logic, no 90-day timer, no dispute queue. A host churns, the next recompute reflects it, and the leaderboard is simply always true. Build it as a ledger and you inherit every one of those problems permanently.

#### 8.7.3 Campaign settings — configurable per campaign

| Setting | Notes |
|---|---|
| Name, slug, public leaderboard URL | |
| Start and end date | |
| Eligible partners | All · tagged group · explicit invite list |
| Eligible referrals | All-time · referred within window · **activated within window** (recommended default) |
| **Scoring mode** | **`total`** (score = all currently-active listings) or **`net_change`** (score = listings gained within the window). Monthly campaigns use `net_change`; the main race uses `total`. This one setting is what stops a runaway leader killing engagement — see §8.3b. |
| Point weights | Defaults to 1 for `listing_published`. Conversion weights exist in the schema, set to 0 for this campaign (§8.3c). |
| Count only active hosts | Default **on** |
| Each listing counts separately | Default **on** |
| Placing prizes | Cash amount + permanent commission floor, per placing |
| Milestone prizes | "First to N points" · "any partner reaching N" |
| Tie-breaker | **Earliest to reach the final score.** Needs to exist before day one, not after a dispute. |
| Leaderboard visibility | Public · partners-only · hidden |

#### 8.7.4 Where commission connects

Prizes grant a **permanent commission floor** on the partner record. The ladder in §4.4 keeps running underneath it:

> **Effective rate = MAX(ladder rate, highest prize floor ever won)**

So a partner sitting at 10% who wins first place moves permanently to 20%, and still climbs to 25% when their book crosses R50,000. Floors stack by taking the highest, never by adding. They attach to the partner, are permanent, and are non-transferable if they sell their group.

#### 8.7.5 The payoff — the R40,000 pool becomes four concurrent campaigns

Every momentum prize in §8.4 stops being a special rule and becomes a campaign:

| Campaign | Window | Eligible | Prizes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Founding Race** | 8 months | All partners · `total` | R15,000 / R7,000 / R3,000 + floors of 20% / 15% / 12% |
| **Monthly Leaderboard** | Rolling 1 month × 8 | All partners · **`net_change`** | R1,000 top scorer |
| **Fast Start** | First 30 days per partner | All partners · `total` | R1,000 to anyone reaching 5 points |
| **First to 10** | Until claimed | All partners · `total` | R2,000, once |

Four campaigns, one engine, one scoring model, zero bespoke logic. And every future push — a December sprint, a Garden Route regional campaign, a "bring 5 in March" — is a config entry, not a build.

#### 8.7.6 What to build now vs. later

**Do not build a campaign platform before you have a campaign.** Minimum viable for launch:

1. **Nightly recompute job** — the core. Everything else is presentation.
2. **Public leaderboard page** — must be live and trustworthy from day one. A competition whose score partners can't see is a competition they stop playing. This is the piece not to compromise on.
3. **Partner dashboard panel** — my score, my rank, my current rate, points to the next commission rung. That last figure is the one that drives behaviour.
4. **Campaign config** — fine as config-in-code for the first run. An admin UI can wait.

**Defer:** concurrent-campaign UI, campaign templates, partner-facing campaign history. Design the schema to allow them; don't build them yet.

## 10. THE MARKETING PACK

Built once, used by everyone, refreshed monthly. **No website builder content until it ships.**

1. **The Commission Maths post** — highest-reach asset in the master strategy. *"You did R60,000 last month. Booking.com took R9,000. Here's what that is over a year."*
2. **The commission calculator** — host enters monthly revenue, sees annual loss. The link partners share. It does the persuading.
3. **Announcement post** — the partner introducing Wielo in their own voice, with a "why I'm doing this" angle.
4. **Looking For post** — *"A guest is looking for a 4-sleeper in Clarens, 12–14 September, R2,000 a night. Right now. LekkeSlaap would charge you 15% to send them. Wielo charges nothing."* **This is the beta's hero asset.**
5. **Guest data post** — *"Booking.com gives you a first name. Wielo gives you their name, email, phone, and every booking they've made."*
6. **Beta honesty post** — *"It's a beta. Some things are rough. Here's the public board where you tell us what to fix, and here's what we shipped last week."* Links to the Build Board. Counter-intuitively one of the strongest trust assets you have.
7. **Build Board win posts** — from month 2, ongoing. *"[Host] asked for it. [N] hosts voted. It's live."* Renewable, credible, free.
8. **Website builder launch post** — held back until it ships, then released to all partners simultaneously as a scheduled beat.
9b. **Comment reply bank** — the 15 questions every group will ask, with approved answers. Prevents guessing and prevents overselling.
10. **Host proof posts** — from month 2, real screenshots from real Founding Hosts. These replace everything above once they exist.

**Rules:** locked strapline on every asset. No banned-list jargon — no "seamless," no "SaaS," no "channel manager." Rand amounts, not percentages, wherever possible. Each partner gets a version with their landing page URL pre-filled.

## 12. RISKS

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **"Beta" reads as a marketing wrapper** | The Build Board and changelog make it structurally, publicly true. Ship every two weeks. Credit hosts by name. Decline things openly with reasons. |
| **Free hosts don't convert** | Individual Decision Window with a real consequence. Build Night in week 1. First-booking guarantee. Monthly contact. Price framed as reward for being early, never as a discount. |
| **Missing website builder kills the pitch** | Lead with Looking For and zero commission. Recruit hosts who already have a web presence. Hold the builder as a mid-beta campaign beat. |
| **A partner posts fake website mockups** | Approved-assets-only rule, written into the agreement. Comment reply bank. Termination clause. |
| **Looking For has no guest demand at launch** | **The largest risk in the plan.** Guest-side group partners, Meta ads to guests from day one, host guest-list invitations, directory SEO. Hold the Looking For marketing asset until week 4–6 so partners never lead with an empty board. |
| **Guests post and get no reply** | Worse than no request at all — the guest never returns and tells people. Never ship the guest flow without the host quote loop in §7.5. Watch the 2-quotes-in-24h metric weekly and stop demand spend if it drops below 40%. |
| **Ad spend wasted on a registration-first page** | Do not run the R5k until the §7.3 flow and §7.5 host loop are live. The current page would give both a poor result and a misleading read on demand. |
| **Build Board signal drowns in guest votes** | Role-tag every account. Weight and surface host votes on host features. Segment guest-facing items. |
| **Masking contradiction thrown back at you** | Pre-publish the explanation: contact details masked until the guest accepts a quote is guest privacy, not host lock-in. Wielo never masks guest data from the host after a booking. Put it in the comment reply bank. |
| **Support load buries you — the main uncapped risk** | Weekly Build Nights instead of 1:1. Founding Host peer group as first-line support. Help centre built from week-1 questions. Stated 48h response expectation set on day one. |
| **Build Board becomes a graveyard** | Seed 20–30 items. 7-day response SLA. Ship visibly every two weeks. "Not doing" used honestly. |
| **Build Board fills with noise** | Points capped at one per host. Moderation. Merge duplicates. Votes inform, don't decide. |
| **Partners go quiet after week 3** | Monthly prize, monthly call, live leaderboard, scheduled asset drops, and the website-builder launch beat. |
| **Discount anchoring — nobody ever pays R999** | Founding price is tied to having been a beta tester, not to asking. Hold this absolutely: the first time you give R599 to someone outside the beta, R999 stops being a real price. **And R999 must be live and transactable from day one.** |
| **The lifetime promise becomes unprofitable** | R599 locked forever on a product that keeps expanding is a real exposure. **Bound it in writing before anyone is briefed:** the lock covers their tier as it exists and everything added *to that tier*. New tiers (Pro, Agency) and genuinely separate products — payments, transaction-based services, paid add-ons — are outside it. Say this once, clearly, in the Founding Host terms. Not saying it is how you end up with a cohort you can't serve profitably in year three. |
| **Group owner sells or abandons the group** | Commission locked to the person, not the group. Non-transferable clause. |
