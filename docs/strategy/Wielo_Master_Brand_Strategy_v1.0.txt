Brand Archetype — The Mark & Pearson Model
  PRIMARY ARCHETYPE  —  MASTERY QUADRANT
  The Outlaw
  Core desire: to overturn what is not working.  Core fear: powerlessness, complicity with a broken system.
  SECONDARY ARCHETYPE  —  BELONGING QUADRANT
  The Everyman  — makes Wielo warm, SA-local, accessible
The Outlaw gives Wielo its mission and its anger. The Everyman gives it warmth and accessibility. Together: principled but approachable. Righteous but never preachy. Bold but never arrogant.
Why The Outlaw:  The named enemy (OTA commission). The freedom frame ("your guests"). The founding story (insider who saw exploitation and fixed it). The values (Ownership, Transparency, Empowerment). The competitive frame ("we exist to make that behaviour unnecessary."). All Outlaw.
ICP Archetypes
Elsa (Tier 1) The Caregiver
Core desire: to protect and nurture. She built a guesthouse because she loves looking after people. Needs safety from Wielo, not rebellion. Liberation framed as safety: "you deserve to own your guest relationships."
Pieter (Tier 2) The Ruler + Hero
Core desire: to create order and control his domain. The OTA commission is a tax on his kingdom. Needs sovereignty and premium quality. Liberation framed as sovereignty: "stop letting agents take a third of your kingdom."
Thandi (Tier 3) The Sage + Creator
Core desire: to find truth through analysis and build something of enduring value. Already knows the problem. Needs evidence-based leverage. Liberation framed as proof: "here is the data that proves it works."
Critical rule:  Wielo does not change its archetype per ICP. What changes is how liberation is framed — as safety for Elsa, sovereignty for Pieter, evidence for Thandi. Same brand. Three emotional registers.

OTAs mask guest data. Airbnb&apos;s Off-Platform Policy (May 2025) prohibits hosts from sharing their own website URL before booking confirmation. We believe this is wrong.
3.  Professional tools should not require a developer.
The assumption that hosts need a tech team to have a professional online presence is false. Wielo disproves it in 10 minutes.
4.  South Africa deserves software built for South Africa.
Rand. Paystack. SA channels. Local support. The global players did not solve this. We did.
5.  Simplicity is a form of respect.
A complicated product does not trust the person using it. Simplicity is not dumbing down — it is eliminating the unnecessary.
6.  A curated directory is worth more than a large one.
Quality over volume, always.
7.  The direct booking shift in SA is already happening — and it is irreversible.
SA ranked in the top two direct booking markets globally in 2025 (SiteMinder, 125M reservations). We are the infrastructure for a shift that is underway.

  PART D
  The Three Problems Wielo Solves
  The core product logic — each backed by market data
Every product decision, every marketing message, and every sales conversation maps to one of these three problems.
  PROBLEM 1
  Expensive success fees are eating your revenue.
  WIELO SOLUTION →  0% success fees. Pure subscription. Keep every rand.
OTAs charge 10–25% commission on every booking. On R60,000/month in bookings, that is R6,000–R15,000 gone before a single expense is paid. A guesthouse paying 15% Booking.com commission on R60,000/month loses R108,000/year — to a company that has never met their guests.

  PROBLEM 2
  You are running a business without knowing your customers.
  WIELO SOLUTION →  Full access to every guest — details, history, and direct messaging.
When a guest books through Booking.com or Airbnb, the host receives their first name and arrival date. The OTA keeps the email, the phone number, the booking history, and the relationship. Airbnb&apos;s Off-Platform Policy (May 2025) now actively prohibits hosts from sharing their own website URL before a booking is confirmed.
“Airbnb now prohibits hosts from sharing contact information or linking to external websites before a booking is confirmed.”  — CTHA Cape Town Hospitality Analysis, May 2026
Wielo answer:  Every guest who books through Wielo gives the host their full name, email, phone number, and booking history. The guest list becomes the host&apos;s most valuable business asset.
  PROBLEM 3
  A website, a directory, and a booking system are three separate expenses.
  WIELO SOLUTION →  One subscription. Website + directory + booking management. Bundled.
Most SA hosts use a Facebook page as their "website," a Booking.com listing for discovery, and a manual calendar on their phone. The average SA web designer charges R8,000–R35,000+ to build a website the host cannot manage themselves. Then they still need a separate booking engine and directory.
Wielo answer:  One subscription bundles a professional property website (live in 10 minutes), a Wielo directory listing (zero commission), and a full booking management system. All connected. All in one place.
What you pay Booking.com in one month  >  What you pay Wielo in one year. That is the single most powerful truth in the Wielo arsenal. Lead with it everywhere.

  PART I
  Product Priorities
  Build sequence informed by research and competitor gaps
These priorities are derived from competitor gaps, verified host complaints across independent review platforms, and what drives word-of-mouth growth.
  PRIORITY 1  BUILD NOW
  The Property Website Must Be Genuinely Beautiful in Under 10 Minutes
This is the word-of-mouth trigger. NightsBridge requires hosts to already have a website. Most Tier 1 hosts use Facebook. Wielo&apos;s website is the feature that visibly and immediately changes their professional standing.
Research basis:  NightsBridge: no website builder. Lodgify: USD, no SA channels. Web agencies: R8k–R35k, 4–12 weeks.
  PRIORITY 2  BUILD NOW
  Guest Data as a Visible Celebrated Feature
When a guest books, the host receives full contact details immediately and visibly — front and centre. Not buried in settings. Consider: "Maria Joubert just booked 3 nights. Her email is maria@gmail.com. She is yours." This is the emotional core of the Wielo value proposition made tangible.
Research basis:  Booking.com masks data. Airbnb prohibits sharing contact details pre-booking (May 2025).
  PRIORITY 3  BUILD NOW
  Mobile-First Guest Booking Experience
The most verified complaint about NightsBridge across Hotel Tech Report, Capterra, and GetApp: the booking engine is not mobile-optimised. One SA owner reported 90–95% of guests book on mobile. This is the clearest product gap to exploit.
Research basis:  "The booking platform is not mobile-friendly for guests. This leads to lost bookings." — Verified SA property owner, Hotel Tech Report 2024
  PRIORITY 4  BUILD NEXT
  "First Direct Booking" Onboarding Flow
63.4% of independent property bookings globally still go through OTAs. Most SA hosts have never run a direct booking strategy. If a host gets no direct booking in 30 days, they leave. Build obsessively toward one booking in the first 14 days.
Research basis:  Churn risk is the primary risk in the research. Onboarding is as important as the product.
  PRIORITY 5  DO NOT BUILD FIRST
  Channel Manager
The first Wielo customer is Elsa — using Facebook as her website with no booking system at all. The channel manager serves a later-stage customer. Build for Elsa first.
Research basis:  NightsBridge already serves channel management for the multi-OTA host. Wielo&apos;s first customer does not have this problem yet.

  Open Questions

These questions do not have answers yet. They need decisions. This section exists so they are not forgotten.
Subscription pricing per tier
R/month for Wielo One, Pro, and Agency. The research suggests R800–R1,500/month for single property positions Wielo at <2% of annual commission cost — the highest-ROI price point.
Payment gateways at launch
